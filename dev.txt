  Commits #4 and #5 are dated January–February 2020, but their parent (#3) and child (#6) are both dated January 2021 — roughly 11 months later. A commit can't legitimately be authored before its parent; these two
  timestamps violate ancestry. Almost certainly the machine's clock was wrong (set to 2020) when those two were committed, or the surrounding commits were re-dated by a later rebase/cherry-pick while these kept their
  stale dates.
  
  Why the screenshot looks scrambled: GitLens sorts the graph by commit date, not topologically. So the two 2020-dated commits sink to the bottom (they look "oldest"), and v0.0 "Add versions script" — which is actually
  the root commit (git rev-list --max-parents=0 confirms it has no parent) — floats up into the middle. The visual order no longer matches the parent chain.

  So the two strange things, concretely:
  1. The v0.0 "Add versions script" commit is the repo root, yet appears mid-graph rather than at the bottom.
  2. That's caused by 989c6e3 and a21feba carrying 2020 dates sandwiched between 2021 ancestors and descendants.
  
  Nothing is corrupted — the DAG is intact; only the dates are inconsistent. Fixing it would mean rewriting history (e.g. a git filter-repo/rebase --committer-date-is-author-date to correct those two timestamps), which
  would change every commit hash from 989c6e3 onward and require a force-push since origin/master is in sync. I'd only do that on your say-so. Want me to, or leave it as a historical curiosity?
